## Operator decisions baked into these plans

- **Budget enforcement (003):** hard cap — over-budget rules are dropped
  silently, never warned-and-included. Lowest priority dropped first. At least
  one rule always survives.
- **Dedup precedence (004):** project-local rules **shadow** same-named global
  rules (local fully overrides; no content merging).

## Findings considered and rejected

- **Cross-turn re-injection prevention:** `rulesInjected` at
  `src/runtime.ts:200-205` already guards this. Not a finding.
- **One-time history seeding staleness:** `seededFromHistory` is intentionally
  one-time; context paths are incrementally updated by `tool.execute.before`.
  By-design, not a bug.
- **Verbatim rule injection as a "security" finding:** rules come from trusted
  filesystem directories authored by the maintainer; injection is the plugin's
  purpose. By-design.
- **Mid-rule truncation / summarization under budget:** rejected in favor of
  whole-rule drops — a half markdown rule is worse than a dropped one.
- **External token-counting dependency (tiktoken):** rejected; chars/4
  heuristic is sufficient for budget decisions and avoids a dependency.
